Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) – One Way Sign

1. What does a One Way sign mean?

A One Way Sign indicates that traffic is allowed to flow in only one direction. It helps keep roads safe by guiding drivers the correct way.

2. Where are One Way Signs used?

One Way Signs are commonly found on city streets, parking lots, campuses, and commercial areas to manage traffic and prevent accidents.

3. Can I ignore a One Way Sign?

No. Ignoring a One Way Sign is both dangerous and illegal. Driving the wrong way can lead to accidents and fines.

4. What do R6-1L and R6-1R mean?

These are MUTCD codes for One Way Signs. R6-1L means the arrow points left, and R6-1R means the arrow points right, showing the allowed traffic direction.

5. Are One Way Signs only for streets?

No. They can also be used in parking lots, warehouses, schools, and offices—anywhere traffic needs direction control.

6. What’s the best size for a One Way Sign?

Standard sizes vary, but for streets, 36×12 inches is common. Larger signs may be used in high-traffic areas for better visibility.

7. Can I print my own One Way Sign?

Yes! With a high-quality vector file, you can print it at any size without losing clarity, making it perfect for official or educational use.
